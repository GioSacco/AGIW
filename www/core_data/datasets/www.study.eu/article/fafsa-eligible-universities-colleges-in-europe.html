FAFSA: Eligible universities & colleges in Europe

Author: Denise Cope — Last update: 18 August 2020

If you are from the United States and want to get your degree abroad, you may be eligible for financial aid through the FAFSA program.

Whether or not you are eligible for funding through FAFSA depends on a variety of factors. One of them is whether the overseas university you want to apply to is recognised by the U.S. Department of Education for the FAFSA scheme.

Read more about how FAFSA works for studying abroad

The table below contains all international schools that are eligible, sorted by country. There are two categories: If a university is listed as “eligible,” it means that you can use FAFSA for loans towards your abroad degree. Some universities are classified as “deferment only”: That means you cannot use FAFSA loans for your abroad degree, but any student loan you currently have can be put into deferred status while attending that university, assuming you follow all guidelines.

Countries where you can study with FAFSA

A FAFSA loan or FAFSA grant can support you to to get a degree in a large number of foreign countries. In Europe, almost all the eligible universities are located in the United Kingdom. Additionally, there are many universities in Germany where you can study to defer payments from previous study loans. Here is an overview:

What if I don’t see my desired university on the FAFSA-approved list?

If you do not see your desired abroad university on the approved FAFSA list, it means that you cannot use US federal loans for the program. You may still be able to receive financial aid through other channels.
